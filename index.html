More Than 200 Volunteers Join Wellington Waterfront Weekend Clean-up

Published: April 23, 2026 Location: Wellington Waterfront Reporter: Local Community Desk

On Saturday morning, volunteers from Wellington neighborhoods and schools carried out a large-scale waterfront clean-up, collecting around 1.2 tonnes of recyclable and non-recyclable waste. Organizers said plastic packaging and disposable cup lids remain the most common items found along the coast.

The clean-up route stretched from Oriental Bay to Taranaki Street Wharf, with six waste-sorting stations set up along the way. The city council's environmental team provided on-site guidance and handed out reusable shopping bags to residents. After the event, organizers called on local businesses to further reduce single-use plastics and support a more consistent community sustainability program.
